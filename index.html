SOUTHEAST ASIA COUNTRIES HAPPINESS REPORT 2021

This Visualization is focused on the Happiness Report Based on the Ladder Score among Southeast Asia countries in 2021

OVERVIEW OF LADDERSCORE WORLDWIDE

Below is an choroplethmap visualized using the Ladder Score from different Countries Worldwide. As we can see, Finland is ranked first for being the Happiest Country with a Ladder Score of 7.842

Happiness Ranking for Southeast Asia Countries

From the chart above, we can see that Singapore having the highest Ladder Score and Myanmar having the lowest Ladder score. Additionally, we can see that the ladder scores of Singapore, Thailand, Philippines, and Vietnam are greater than the 2021 average ladder score of 4.426.

Happiness Breakdown for Southeast Asia Countries

Below is a breakdown of the Factors that affects the Happiness Score in Southeast Asia countries. As seen in the graph above, GDP per capita is a metric that significantly affects life ladder rankings. Overall, all of the Southeast Asian nations have very low scores for generosity.

Changes in Happiness Ranking from 2010-2020

According to the bump chart, the Philippines' life ladder has gone up from 6 to 3 since 2010. In the meantime, Malaysia and Myanmar's position on the life ladder has substantially declined. Except for 2010, when it fell to rank 2, Singapore has been remarkably steady in maintaining its top position.

Happiness Breakdown for Singapore

As we can see from the Bump chart beside, Singapore has been remarkably steady in maintaining at the top. Here we will breakdown the Factor that contributes to the Happiness score. From the piechart below, we can see that Log GDP contributes the most, 33.91% to the Happiness score and generosity contributes the least to the score, 3.52%.
